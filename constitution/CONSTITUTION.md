# Constitution of the Contemporary Sanskrit Observatory

**Version:** 1.0 (Genesis)

---

## 1. Mission

The Contemporary Sanskrit Observatory (CSO) is a computational research infrastructure dedicated to documenting, preserving, organizing, and analysing contemporary Sanskrit communication.

Its purpose is to create a durable, reproducible, and extensible scholarly resource that supports linguistic, anthropological, historical, and computational research.

---

## 2. Scope

The Observatory documents contemporary Sanskrit communication across multiple communicative domains, including but not limited to:

- Broadcast news
- Spoken conversation
- Public lectures
- Interviews
- Sports commentary
- Children's media
- Film
- Literature
- Digital media
- Future communicative forms

The Observatory is intentionally open-ended. New collections may be added as contemporary Sanskrit evolves.

---

## 3. Principles

The Observatory is governed by the following principles.

### Evidence First

Every observation must be traceable to documented evidence.

### Reproducibility

All computational analyses should be reproducible from curated source materials.

### Openness

Metadata standards, ontologies, and software should be openly documented whenever possible.

### Versioning

Collections, ontologies, and documentation are versioned to preserve institutional memory.

### Stewardship

The Observatory exists to preserve knowledge for future scholarship rather than for individual projects alone.

---

## 4. Collections

The Observatory is organised into versioned collections.

Each collection represents a coherent corpus of communicative events sharing common characteristics.

The first registered collection is:

**CSO001 — Contemporary Sanskrit Broadcast News (Vārtā)**

Future collections will include conversational Sanskrit, sports commentary, children's media, literature, and other communicative domains.

---

## 5. Core Entities

The Observatory recognises the following primary entities.

- Collection
- Institution
- Person
- Event
- Artifact
- Document
- Observation
- Knowledge Object

Relationships between these entities are defined within the repository ontology.

---

## 6. Governance

The Constitution evolves through versioned revisions.

Changes should be infrequent, documented, and justified.

Operational procedures, software implementations, and analytical methods may evolve independently without requiring constitutional revision.

---

## 7. Long-term Vision

The Contemporary Sanskrit Observatory serves as the first operational Observatory within the broader Computational Mouseion.

Its architecture is intended to support comparative observatories for additional languages, traditions, and cultural domains while maintaining common metadata standards and computational principles.
